01 · About

Why Plumbline exists.

Plumbline is a calm, observational surface for the person who already has a portfolio — and already knows, in the rough sense, what is in it — and would like to read it. Read it across the macro weather that is quietly moving it. Read it against the news tone that is shifting around it. Read it on a Tuesday morning in three minutes, against a frame that holds, before the day starts pulling your attention in nine other directions.

03 · What we believe

Five convictions the product is built on.

These are not slogans. They are decisions that shape the design, the copy, and what the product refuses to do.

i.

A Steady Pulse means the underlying signals are aligned. Don't move. When the twelve factors agree — when the price behaviour, the macro context (including the Bank of Canada overnight rate when it matters), and the news tone are pointing the same way — Pulse reads Steady. That is not a signal to act. It is a signal that nothing is asking for your attention. The hardest discipline in active investing is the discipline of not moving when nothing has changed. Plumbline is built to make that discipline easier, by giving you a read that says, in plain terms, nothing has changed, and the absence of change is the information.

ii.

A Watch Pulse means something has shifted. Read more before you act. A Watch band — Light, Active, Strong, or Intense — means at least one of the twelve factors has moved enough to register. That is the moment to read, not the moment to transact. Read the Daily Brief. Read the factor breakdown. Read the news tone. Read what the same signal was doing a week ago. If after reading the picture is clear, act. If it is not, do not. Pulse tells you when the picture has changed. It does not tell you what to do about it. That gap is intentional, and it is the part of the product you will be most grateful for, three years from now, when you look back on the times the urge to act was strongest and the picture was least clear.

iii.

Markets reward consistency, not frequency. The investor who checks their book once a day, in a calm voice, and acts only when the read justifies the action, will tend to make better decisions over a decade than the one who checks nine times a day and acts on every twitch. The data on this is older than any of us. Plumbline's whole product is built to slow you down enough to be consistent — to give you one read per day that is worth the read, in a voice that does not push you toward a transaction, with a vocabulary that lets you hold a position with confidence when holding is the right call. Frequency is a product of design, not of markets. Plumbline is designed for less frequency and more rigour. That is the bet.

iv.

The 12 factors are not a secret. We document all of them. Tap any Pulse score, and you see the factors that pushed it up and the factors that pulled it down. The factors are not hidden behind a paywall, are not described in vague terms, and are not the proprietary moat the product pretends to defend. The moat, if there is one, is the discipline of blending twelve factors consistently across every position in your book, every day, in a voice you can read. The factors themselves — what they measure, how they are computed, what their inputs are — are documented in full inside the product. If you want to disagree with a factor's weight, you can. Disagreement, reasoned, is part of how the read gets better over time.

v.

Your decisions are yours. Pulse surfaces what it sees, nothing more. Plumbline will never tell you to buy, to sell, to trim, to add, or to hold. The product earns its value by being trustworthy, and a product that whispers buy or sell stops being trustworthy the moment it does. The Pulse score is observational. The Daily Brief is observational. The product, as a whole, is observational. What you do with the read is yours, made on the basis of your own book, your own horizon, your own tolerance for risk, your own registered-account rules (RRSP, TFSA, FHSA, or non-registered), and any advice you have received from a qualified professional.

04 · How Pulse works

One score, twelve factors, five bands.

The longer methodology lives in the Pulse explainer on the home page; this is the short version.

Pulse is a number from 0 to 100 that sits on every holding in your portfolio, in one of five named bands: Quiet, Light, Active, Strong, Intense. The number is not a price. It is not a forecast. It is a single, blended read on how much attention a position deserves from you today, given the conditions around it.

The score is built from twelve factors. Some of them come from the market itself — price movement over different windows, how a holding has behaved relative to its own history, how it has behaved relative to the sector it lives in, how its volatility has changed over the recent past. Some come from the wider world — Bank of Canada overnight rate conditions, currency moves, commodity pressures, the kind of macro weather that quietly shapes every position in a book. And some come from the news — drawn from a curated set of Canadian and global financial and geopolitical sources, weighted through the GDELT index for tone and direction over a rolling window, so the news factor reflects shift rather than any single story.

No single factor drives the score. The twelve are blended together, weighted by how much each one tends to matter in the current environment, and the result is the number you see on the screen. When you tap on a Pulse score, the factors that pushed it up or pulled it down are listed for you, in the real term and a one-line definition the first time. Nothing is hidden. Nothing is dressed up. You can see the ingredients, and you can disagree with the outcome.

The five bands are observational, not directional. A Strong Pulse means conditions are moving. It does not mean the position is a buy. A Quiet Pulse means nothing is asking for your attention. It does not mean the position is safe. The bands describe the conditions. What the conditions mean for you, given your own circumstances, is your call.

What Pulse does not do is just as important as what it does. Pulse does not predict. It does not forecast. It does not rank holdings against each other in a way that implies one is better than another. The score has no opinion on whether you should act. It has only an opinion on whether the conditions around a position are worth noticing today.

The asset universe is the full S&P 500, the top ten cryptocurrencies by market capitalisation, and ten major commodities — gold, silver, oil, natural gas, wheat, corn, soybeans, copper, platinum, and lithium. Canadian-listed names (TSX and TSXV) are in scope. Most major U.S. names are interlisted on TSX in CAD; Plumbline shows you the price on the exchange you hold the position on, in the currency you hold it in, and the data layer handles the FX. The picture is the whole picture.
