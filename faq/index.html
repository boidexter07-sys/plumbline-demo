02 · I

About Pulse.

What Pulse is, how it works, and the things it deliberately refuses to do.

01What is Pulse, in real terms?

Pulse is a number from 0 to 100 that sits on every holding in your portfolio, with a band name attached. The bands are Quiet, Light, Active, Strong, and Intense. The number describes how much attention the position is asking for from you today, given the market conditions around it and the world around the market. It is built from twelve different inputs — some about price, some about the wider economy, some about the news — that are blended into a single read. The number changes through the week as the conditions change. It is a description of what is happening now, not a forecast of what will happen next.

02How is Pulse different from a stock rating or analyst recommendation?

An analyst rating — the kind you might see on a brokerage research page — is an opinion about whether a stock is worth buying, holding, or selling, expressed as Buy / Hold / Sell or as a star rating. Pulse is none of those things. Pulse does not tell you whether a stock is a buy. Pulse tells you whether the conditions around a stock are asking for your attention today. A position can sit at Strong Pulse for a week because of macro turbulence, and the analyst rating can remain Buy, and both can be true at the same time — they are answering different questions. The analyst rating answers is this a good long-term position. Pulse answers is something happening around this position right now that I should read carefully before I act.

03How is Pulse different from Morningstar, Seeking Alpha, or the brokerage research feed?

Morningstar gives you a long-term fundamental view: a star rating, a fair value estimate, a moat (a structural advantage a company has over its competitors) assessment. Useful, slow, and not what Pulse is doing. Seeking Alpha is a wall of contributor opinion — useful as a source of opinion, less useful as a frame for your own book. The brokerage research feed is whatever your broker chooses to surface, optimised for engagement with their product. Pulse is none of those. Pulse is a daily, quantitative read on the conditions around the positions you actually hold, in a frame that is the same for every holding and every user, on a methodology that is fully documented. The difference is the frame: Pulse is bound to your book, refreshed daily, and explained in full.

04Why five bands? Why not just "good" or "bad"?

Because the conditions around a holding are not a one-dimensional thing, and forcing them into "good" or "bad" throws away the nuance the score is meant to capture. The five bands describe how much is happening, not whether it is good or bad. A Quiet position can be quietly excellent or quietly in trouble — the band tells you the score has nothing noteworthy to report. A Strong position can be Strong for favorable reasons or unfavorable ones. The bands are observational. They tell you what the conditions look like, and they let you bring the judgment about what those conditions mean.

05What does a Watch band mean — should I act on it?

A Watch band (Light, Active, Strong, or Intense) means conditions around the position are asking for some level of attention, in increasing degrees of seriousness. None of them are a signal to do anything. The bands do not have opinions on whether you should buy, sell, trim, or hold. They have opinions on whether the conditions around the position are worth noticing. Reading carefully, in your own time, is the right response. Acting on a band reading — without doing the read — is exactly the kind of hurried decision the product is built to discourage. 06What does a Steady band mean — should I be doing nothing?

A Steady band (Quiet or Light) means the underlying signals are aligned and nothing is asking for your attention. That is not a signal to act either. It is a signal that the conditions around the position have not shifted enough to warrant a read. The hardest discipline in active investing is the discipline of not moving when nothing has changed. Plumbline is built to make that discipline easier by giving you a read that says, in plain terms, nothing has changed. Holding a Steady position through a Steady week is a feature, not a missed opportunity.

07Does Pulse predict prices?

No. Pulse is a description of present conditions, not a forecast of future ones. The twelve factors are blended using recent history, weighted by how much each tends to matter in the current environment, and the result is a number that reflects what is happening now. The score changes through the week as the inputs change. The score has no opinion about where the price will be next week or next year. Treating Pulse as a forecast would be a category error — Pulse is the read, not the prediction. If you want a forecast, no product can give you a reliable one, and any product that claims to is selling something else.

08Why 12 factors and not 6, or 30?

Twelve is the smallest number that splits the work across price, macro, and news without any one category dominating the score. Fewer factors means the price-derived inputs overwhelm the macro and the news, and Pulse starts to look like a momentum indicator (a metric that measures how strongly a price has been moving in one direction). More factors means the blend gets noisy and the band moves get harder to interpret. Twelve splits the work, keeps each factor legible, and produces a score that is stable enough to be useful and responsive enough to register shifts. The full factor list is documented inside the product, in the same place you see the factor breakdown for any individual Pulse score, and in our public spec at pulse-legend-spec.md.

09How often does Pulse update?

The underlying factors refresh on different cadences, depending on what they measure. Price-related factors refresh through each trading session. Macro factors refresh on the schedule of the data sources — daily for some indicators, intraday for others. News factors refresh as new stories surface, weighted for tone and direction over a rolling window. The Pulse number on a holding may move several times in a given week, and may sit still for a day or two at a stretch. There is no benefit to watching it tick by tick. The score is meant to be read once a day, not once a minute.

10Can I see the factors behind any Pulse score?

Yes. Tap any Pulse score on any holding, and the twelve factors expand. Each factor shows its current reading, its recent direction, and a one-line description of what it measures. You can see which factors pushed the score up and which pulled it down. Nothing is hidden. Nothing is dressed up. If you want to disagree with a factor's weight or with the blend, the factors are documented in full in the methodology section, and you have the vocabulary to make the disagreement reasoned.

On the 12 factors. The legend on the home page (How Pulse works) lists all 12 factors with their colour, glyph, category, and one-line definition. You can also read the full specification — including the strength axis and the type axis for filtering — in pulse-legend-spec.md.
02 · II

About your account.

Sign-up, the trial, billing, cancellation, exports, and what we do — and do not do — with your portfolio.

11How do I sign up?

You create an account with an email address and a password. That is the entire sign-up. There is no questionnaire about your investing experience, no income verification, no documents to upload. The product is for observation only — it does not hold your money or place orders — so the account requirements are the minimum needed to keep your portfolio tied to a sign-in you control. Once the account exists, you can build your portfolio by entering the tickers you hold, and the product does the rest.

12Will Plumbline work with my RRSP, TFSA, or FHSA?

Yes. Plumbline is a read-only analytical surface, and it is account-agnostic. You enter your positions manually — by ticker, by position size, by which account type they live in (RRSP, TFSA, FHSA, or non-registered) — and the product shows you a Pulse score on every position. You can tag holdings by account type inside the product, so you can see a Pulse breakdown for your RRSP separately from your TFSA separately from your non-registered book, if that view helps you read your own positions. Plumbline does not connect to your brokerage, does not request your brokerage credentials, and does not have read access to any of your accounts. Your accounts stay where they are. You stay in control.

13Does Plumbline handle U.S.-listed holdings?

Yes. The asset universe includes the full S&P 500, and most major U.S. names are also interlisted on TSX in CAD — Apple, Microsoft, the megacaps, and many of the names you would actually want to hold. Plumbline shows you the price on the exchange you hold the position on, in the currency you hold it in. If your Apple position sits inside your RRSP at Questrade in CAD, that is the price and currency you see. If you hold the same ticker on the USD leg at IBKR Canada, that is what you see instead. The data layer handles the FX translation; you do not need to think about it.

14Is the subscription in CAD?

Yes. The subscription is $4.99 CAD per month, billed monthly in Canadian dollars through Stripe. The 7-day free trial is also billed in CAD — you will not see a USD conversion on your statement, and there is no FX surprise at checkout. Stripe handles all payment processing in CAD; Plumbline never touches your card number, CVV, or expiration date.

15What does the 7-day trial include?

The full product. There is no feature gating and no premium tier waiting in the wings. During the seven days, you can build your portfolio with as many holdings as you like, watch Pulse move on your real positions, read the Daily Brief each morning, use the sandbox, browse the Markets Now tile, and look at the Leaderboard. No payment information is collected before the seventh day, and you will not be charged until day eight. If you cancel before the trial ends, you will not be charged at all.

16Can I cancel anytime? Will I be charged if I cancel during the trial?

You can cancel from the account settings page at any moment, for any reason or no reason. If you cancel during the seven-day trial, you will not be charged — the trial ends the moment you cancel, and the account reverts to read-only. If you cancel after the trial, the subscription ends at the end of the billing cycle in which you cancel, and you are not charged again. There is no retention call, no exit survey, no friction. The product is designed to be worth keeping on its merits, and the cancellation path is the proof of that.

17Is my portfolio data safe?

Plumbline does not connect to your brokerage, does not hold your account credentials, and does not place orders on your behalf. The portfolio you build inside the product is held in encrypted storage under your account. Authentication is handled by a third-party identity provider (Clerk); payment is handled by Stripe; we do not see or store your card number. The full data handling practices are laid out in the privacy policy linked in the footer, and the short version is this: the data is yours, it stays tied to your account, and you can delete it at any time.

18Can I export my data or delete my account?

Both. From the settings page, you can export your portfolio, your Pulse history, your sandbox positions, and your Daily Brief archive as standard CSV or JSON files, in formats portable enough to load into a spreadsheet, another tool, or your own records. You can also delete the account outright, which removes the portfolio, the history, and the associated data within a short window. If you cancel your subscription without deleting the account, your data remains exportable for ninety days after cancellation, and is then permanently deleted.

19Will Plumbline report my holdings to the CRA?

No. Plumbline is a read-only analytical surface for your own use. We do not file anything on your behalf, do not report your holdings or transactions to the Canada Revenue Agency, and do not generate tax slips of any kind. You remain responsible for your own tax reporting — including T1135 (the Foreign Income Verification Statement, required if you hold more than $100,000 CAD in foreign property at any time in the year) and T1134 (the inter-affiliate withholding tax return, if it applies to your situation) — and we recommend working with a qualified tax professional for any cross-border or registered-account questions specific to your circumstances.

20Do you offer an annual plan, family plan, or student discount?

No. The product is $4.99 CAD a month, billed monthly, with one trial at the front. There is no annual plan, no family plan, no student discount, no enterprise tier. The single tier is a deliberate choice — the product is the same for everyone who subscribes, at the same price, with the same surface. If a future version of Plumbline needs more tiers, the tiers will appear in the pricing, not in a footnote.

02 · III

About the data.

Where the prices come from, where the news comes from, and the universe the product covers.

21Where do Plumbline's prices come from?

Prices come from a small set of reputable market data providers, aggregated and normalised before they reach the product. We do not pull from a single source, because a single source is a single point of failure. The providers we use are referenced in the privacy policy, and the data layer is abstracted so that we can change providers without changing what you see. Prices are delivered on a delay appropriate to a retail subscription — slightly delayed relative to a paid trading terminal — and the delay is consistent across every position in the product.

22How current is the news?

The Markets Now tile and the news-related Pulse factors draw from a curated feed of major financial and geopolitical sources — Canadian (Globe and Mail, Financial Post, BNN Bloomberg, CBC Business, The Logic) and global — indexed through GDELT (the Global Database of Events, Language, and Tone). The headlines are surfaced within minutes of publication; the tone analysis that feeds Pulse runs on a rolling window that captures the last several days, so the news factor reflects direction and shift rather than any single story. The product is not a real-time newswire, and it is not trying to be — the goal is to give you the stories that matter, when they matter, without the noise that surrounds them on a media site.

23What sources are in the macro layer?

Rates, currencies, commodity prices, and broad sector indices, drawn from a small set of public and licensed providers. The Bank of Canada overnight rate is a primary input for the Canadian rate factor; the macro layer also covers global rate moves, currency crosses (CAD/USD, CAD/EUR, CAD/JPY), commodity pressures, and broad sector rotation. The exact provider list is referenced in the methodology section. The macro layer is the part of the model most likely to change as data sources evolve — the what (rates, currencies, commodities, sectors) is more stable than the where (which provider feeds which input). Plumbline commits to documenting the what in full, even when the where changes quietly underneath.

24What's in the asset universe?

Plumbline covers the full S&P 500, the top ten cryptocurrencies by market capitalisation, and ten major commodities — gold, silver, oil, natural gas, wheat, corn, soybeans, copper, platinum, and lithium. Canadian-listed names (TSX and TSXV) are in scope. Most major U.S. names are also interlisted on TSX in CAD. The list may grow as the product grows. The universe is intentionally broader than the typical brokerage's "stocks and ETFs only" view, because the typical brokerage view does not match the way a real portfolio is actually built. If you hold a commodity or a token, you should be able to see it on the same surface as the rest of your book.

25Does Plumbline see my actual brokerage account?

No. Plumbline does not connect to your brokerage, does not request your brokerage credentials, and does not have read access to your account at Questrade, Wealthsimple, IBKR Canada, or any other institution. The portfolio you see inside the product is the portfolio you enter yourself, by ticker, position size, and optional account-type tag (RRSP / TFSA / FHSA / non-registered). This is by design. Read-only access to brokerage accounts is technically possible, but it raises security and trust questions that Plumbline has decided, deliberately, not to take on. The product is a read-only surface on data you provide. It is not a view into your real brokerage.
